<Title>Taking Control of Heart Failure</Title>
<Subtitle>Questions to Ask</Subtitle>
<Content><p>An echocardiogram is the test most commonly used to evaluate heart function. The test will tell your doctor and you about your heart
and heart valve function. Another test of heart function involves injecting a very small amount of radioactive material into your blood to produce images of your heart. This test is frequently called a radionucleide or MUGA scan.
It is important to know your heart function. Doing so helps guide your care. However, once your doctor has evaluated your heart function, it is not necessary to keep testing it.</p></Content>
