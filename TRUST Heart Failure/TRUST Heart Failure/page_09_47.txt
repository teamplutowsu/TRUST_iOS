<Title>Advance Care Planning</Title>
<Subtitle>Terms Commonly Used in Advance Care Planning</Subtitle>
<Content><p>Hospice: A type of care program that supports patients and their families through the dying process and helps surviving family members through bereavement.<br>
Living will: A legal document that lets a person who is unable to make decisions (usually because of inability to communicate during a terminal illness) express their wishes about lifesustaining treatment.<br>
Palliative care: A type of care program that can be used when a disease is no longer responsive to curative treatment. Palliative care generally involves treatments that seek to soothe, prevent, relieve or reduce symptoms.</p></Content>
